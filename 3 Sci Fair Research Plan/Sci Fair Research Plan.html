Research Plan Instructions

Write a research plan. The plan should include the following:

  • Question or problem
  • Goals/expected outcomes/hypotheses
  • Methods or procedures
  • Types of data to be collected and how it will be analyzed
  • Bibliography (at least five major references)

Detailed Research Plan Instructions

Write and submit the research plan and approval form with parent/guardian signatures for teacher approval. Consult teachers, other adults, or scientists who might help you. Get additional forms signed and approved as needed.

Research Plan Requirements

  1. Rationale: Provide a brief background on your research problem, its importance, and any potential societal impact.
  2. Research Question, Hypothesis, Goals, and Expected Outcomes: Explain how your goals are based on the rationale described above.
  3. Procedures: Detail all procedures and experimental designs, including methods for data collection. Focus only on your project; do not include work done by others.
  4. Risk and Safety: Identify potential risks and safety precautions.
  5. Data Analysis: Describe how you will analyze the data or results.
  6. Bibliography: List at least five major references, such as science journal articles, books, and websites.

Research Plan for Specific Projects

1. Human Participants Research

  • Participants: Describe age range, gender, and racial/ethnic composition. Identify vulnerable populations (e.g., minors, pregnant women, prisoners, economically disadvantaged).
  • Recruitment: Where will participants be found? How will they be invited?
  • Methods: Describe what participants will be asked to do, including surveys, questionnaires, and testing frequency and duration.
  • Risk Assessment: Identify risks such as physical, psychological, social, or legal discomfort. Explain how risks will be minimized and list potential benefits.
  • Protection of Privacy: Explain how personal information will be kept confidential or anonymous. Describe data storage, access, and disposal plans.
  • Informed Consent: Describe how participants will be informed about the study, including their right to withdraw at any time.

2. Vertebrate Animal Research

  • Alternatives: Discuss alternatives to using vertebrate animals and justify their use.
  • Research Impact: Explain the potential impact or contribution of your research.
  • Procedures: Detail procedures, minimizing discomfort, pain, or injury. Include chemical concentrations and drug dosages if applicable.
  • Animal Details: Provide details such as animal species, strain, age, and source. Justify the planned number of animals.
  • Care and Housing: Describe daily care, housing conditions, and plans for animals at the end of the study.

3. Potentially Hazardous Biological Agents Research

  • Source of Organism: Identify the organism source and describe the Biosafety Level (BSL) assessment and determination.
  • Safety Precautions: Detail safety protocols and disposal methods.

4. Hazardous Chemicals, Activities & Devices

  • Risk Assessment: Describe the risk assessment process, supervision, safety measures, and disposal methods.
