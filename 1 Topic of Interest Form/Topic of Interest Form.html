Deciding on a Topic

Read "How To Do A Project" before beginning.

Select a topic of interest and answer the following questions:

  • What topic interests me?
  • What question do I plan to investigate?
  • What materials will be needed?
  • Does this problem require sophisticated or expensive materials?
  • Is the experiment safe to conduct?
  • Is it a valid and testable experiment?
  • Will it be possible to obtain results from this investigation?
  • Does this experiment have significance in today’s society?

I will approve your topic, and then you can move forward with your project.

Choosing a Project Title

State the title in the form of a question, such as:

  • What are... How will... The Effects of... How does...

Students May Submit Projects in the Following Categories:

  • Biological Science A – Animal Science, Medicine and Health Science, Microbiology
  • Biological Science B – Cellular and Molecular Biology, Biochemistry, Behavioral and Social Sciences, Plant Sciences
  • Chemistry – Composition, structure, properties, and reactions of matter
  • Earth Environmental Science – Geology, mineralogy, oceanography, meteorology, climatology, pollution studies
  • Physics – The science of matter, energy, and their interactions
  • Math, Statistics, Data Science
  • Technology/Engineering – Projects applying scientific principles to practical uses

Risks and Safety

Projects involving human subjects or surveys will require approval from a school-based Institutional Review Board (IRB) before experimentation begins. This committee includes a science educator, a school administrator, and an expert in risk evaluation. Refer to the ISEF Official Rules for detailed guidelines.
